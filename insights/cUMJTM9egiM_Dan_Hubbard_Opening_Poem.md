# Overview

## Summary
Dan Hubbard opens the [un]prompted conference with an AI-generated poem warning of the risks inherent in the "vibe coding" and "prompt-driven" era of development.

## Key Takeaway
The dissolution of traditional security perimeters and the rise of "vibe-based" coding require a fundamental shift toward agentic bounding and logging.

# Insights

## The Citizen Developer Surge
- AI has empowered non-technical staff to rapidly build dozens of applications, often bypassing traditional software engineering review gates.
- "Coding by feel" has largely replaced formalized change control, creating a high-velocity environment with minimal security oversight.
- The "thousand apps by end of week" pace within organizations creates a massive, unmanaged shadow IT and security surface.

## Collapse of the Perimeter
- The traditional "castle and wall" security model has fundamentally failed in an era defined by broad, token-based agent identities.
- Traditional network perimeters have been effectively replaced by tokens and prompts that defenders can barely perceive in real-time.
- Digital boundaries have "taken leave," leaving internal resources exposed to deep, autonomous inter-agent communication and data sharing.

## Risks of broad Agentic Scopes
- AI agents frequently operate with excessively broad permissions, allowing them to function as "guards asleep" at the organizational gate.
- Broadly scoped agents allow malicious plug-ins to stealthily slip code into the enterprise environment without triggering traditional alarms.
- High-privilege service accounts with "god mode" in test environments remain a primary and easily exploited oversight in AI systems.

## The Speed of "Vibe" Vulnerabilities
- New vulnerabilities "softly bloom" in CI/CD pipelines, often reaching production before any scanner has had time to analyze them.
- In the GenAI era, "Zero Day" is redefined as the temporal space between a user's prompt and its immediate execution.
- Human-speed review cycles are structurally incompatible with the near-instant deployment of AI-generated code and infrastructure changes.

## Silent Drift and Log Oversight
- Modern breaches hide in the "silent drift" of incrementally shifting permission scopes that occur during rapid, automated agentic workflows.
- Credentials and sensitive PII are frequently abandoned in system logs that lack dedicated human or automated security oversight.
- While AI outputs appear "certain and clean," they are often "catastrophically imprecise" regarding the actual security of the code.

## Redefining the Modern Zero Day
- A zero day is no longer just an unknown binary flaw; it is the "vibe at scale" where haste overrides safety.
- Governance models that arrive after the fact (e.g., via credit check) are useless for stopping the results of misplaced trust.
- The industry is currently prioritizing the speed of "automated haste" over the implementation of durable, verifiable security principles.

## Bounding the Autonomous Agent
- Organizations must proactively "log the agent and bind its reach" to prevent AI-driven automation from becoming a compromise bridge.
- Effective security in 2026 requires an assumption that automation will breach, moving focus toward robust containment and forensic proof.
- Continuous, comprehensive logging is the only way to provide the "logs to prove" security in a world of pervasive automation.

## Securing the "Exploding" Path
- Builders and innovators should be encouraged to explore, provided the paths they travel are architected with security by design.
- Security teams must pivot from being blockers to being facilitators of the secure path where "vibes" and code collide.
- The goal is to enable bold innovation while ensuring the organizational infrastructure remains resilient to the risks of instant code.
