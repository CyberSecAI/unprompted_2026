# Overview

## Summary
Ragini Ramalingam, Enterprise Security Lead at Snowflake, outlines a dynamic governance framework for managing the transition from deterministic to context-aware AI systems.

## Key Takeaway
Modern AI governance must shift from reactive approvals to proactive, business-aligned risk ownership that matches AI innovation velocity.

# Insights

## Deterministic to Context-Aware Shift
- Traditional security assumed deterministic execution where humans defined logic and systems simply performed predefined tasks.
- AI systems make real-time, context-aware decisions at runtime that are no longer strictly controlled by human-defined workflows.
- This shift fundamentally blurs security boundaries across endpoints, networks, and cloud SaaS platforms simultaneously.

## Velocity-Aligned Governance
- Governing AI is described as "laying tracks in front of a running train," requiring speed without causing derailment.
- Traditional, static review cycles are insufficient for the weekly feature release cadence of modern AI vendors.
- Governance models must be redesigned to evolve at the same velocity as the underlying AI technology and experimentation.

## Cross-Functional Steering Committees
- Effective governance requires a committee involving Security, IT, Legal, Privacy, AI/ML, Data Engineering, and Procurement.
- This broad membership ensures visibility into all AI adoption patterns and local business unit usage across the enterprise.
- The committee identifies systemic risks and develops technical, process-level, and contractual mitigation strategies.

## Business-Led Risk Ownership
- Enabling AI is fundamentally a business decision and a business trade-off rather than a purely technical security choice.
- Executive leadership across product, engineering, and sales must proactively own and manage their unit's AI risks.
- Aligning governance with the organization's risk appetite allows security to support, rather than hinder, the corporate innovation DNA.

## Paths to AI Visibility
- Comprehensive governance is impossible without deep visibility into how and where AI tools are being utilized.
- Path-to-visibility include mature procurement oversight, security-gated feature activations, and automated discovery of AI artifacts.
- Rationalizing AI products allows organizations to eliminate redundant tools, reducing both operational overhead and the enterprise attack surface.

## Automated Artifact Discovery
- Snowflake utilizes its own AI Data Cloud and "security lake" to autonomously detect AI interactions and artifacts.
- Analysis of endpoint and network telemetry identifies specialized components like MCP servers, specific models, and active agents.
- Automated discovery provides a continuous, real-time map of an organization's actual AI footprint versus the sanctioned one.

## Strategic Security Partnerships
- Maintaining security partners within every business unit provides critical, early insights into decentralized AI adoption.
- Continuous partnership with business unit peers facilitates risk-informed decision-making during the earliest stages of AI experimentation.
- This collaborative model is essential for effecting the cultural change required for responsible and secure enterprise AI usage.

## Implementing Enterprise Guardrails
- Enterprise guardrails involve deploying specific endpoint, network, and DLP controls tailored to modern AI usage patterns.
- These technical controls must enforce the risk policies established by the steering committee without creating friction.
- Proactive risk ownership transforms governance from a "blocking" function into a scalable enabler of secure AI transformation.
